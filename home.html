A planet is a celestial body that orbits a star, is massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity, and has cleared its orbital path of other objects

Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is a cold, dusty, desert world known as the "Red Planet" due to its rusty-red appearance caused by iron oxide on its surface.

The "red planet" refers to Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, known for its reddish-orange appearance due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

The sun is a star, specifically a yellow dwarf star, located at the center of our solar system, and it's a giant, glowing ball of hot gas, primarily hydrogen and helium, that provides Earth with light and heat.

A planet is a large, rounded astronomical body that is generally required to be in orbit around a star, stellar remnant, or brown dwarf, and is not one itself.

The temperature at the surface of the Sun is about 10,000 Fahrenheit (5,600 Celsius). The temperature rises from the surface of the Sun inward towards the very hot center of the Sun where it reaches about 27,000,000 Fahrenheit (15,000,000 Celsius).

A planet must have cleared its orbital path of other objects of comparable size, meaning its gravity has pulled in or ejected any other significant bodies in its vicinity.
